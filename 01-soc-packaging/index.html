Why Packaging Matters

  • Performance — interconnect latency & bandwidth between die and memory now dominate system bottlenecks
  • Power — off-chip I/O burns 5–10× more energy per bit than on-die interconnect
  • Cost — yield drops exponentially with monolithic die area; chiplets restore economics
  • Integration — heterogeneous process nodes (logic, analog, I/O, memory) on a single package
"Packaging, not chip manufacturing, is now the primary supply constraint for AI accelerators."
— Industry consensus, 2025
Advanced packaging market: ~$45B in 2024 → projected ~$80B by 2030 (CAGR ≈ 9.4%)

Fan-Out Wafer-Level Packaging

Redistribution Layers (RDL) — Cu/PI, 2–5 μm L/S

Solder Balls (fan-out area > die area)

The key innovation: the redistribution layer (RDL) routes signals from the die's fine-pitch pads to a larger fan-out area, enabling more I/Os without increasing die size.

TSMC InFO Variants

  • InFO-WLP — single die, mobile SoCs (Apple A-series since A10)
  • InFO-PoP — Package-on-Package; DRAM stacked above logic
  • InFO-L — large-scale fan-out with local silicon interconnect (LSI) bridges
  • InFO-M — multi-die fan-out for networking ASICs
Fan-out eliminated the package substrate for mobile SoCs, reducing Z-height by ~0.3 mm and improving power integrity.

TSMC SoIC & Intel Foveros

TSMC SoIC

  • SoIC-X — Chip-on-Wafer, face-to-face hybrid bonding (finest pitch, highest density)
  • SoIC-P — Chip-on-Wafer, face-to-back, TSV-based; allows >2 die stacking tiers
  • Combined with CoWoS in CoWoS+SoIC for full 3.5D system integration

  • Foveros Direct — hybrid bond (<10 μm pitch, Cu-Cu)

The Chiplet Rationale

Monolithic Die Problems

  • Yield: At N3, a 600 mm² die may have <30% yield; two 300 mm² chiplets can exceed 70% each
  • Cost: Advanced node wafers cost $20–30K; smaller die = more per wafer
  • Flexibility: Can't mix process nodes on one die (analog ≠ logic ≠ I/O)
  • Reticle limit: Max ~858 mm² per lithography exposure

Chiplet Advantages

  • Yield recovery: Smaller die → quadratic yield improvement
  • Mix & match: Compute at N3, I/O at N5, SerDes at N7 — each optimised
  • IP reuse: Same chiplet across product families (AMD CCD → EPYC, Ryzen, Threadripper)
  • Time-to-market: Parallel development of independent die
AMD's proof point: A single Zen CCD chiplet design is reused across consumer (Ryzen), workstation (Threadripper), and server (EPYC) product lines — varying only the I/O die and chiplet count per package.

Thermal & Power Challenges

PDN integrity · TDP scaling · Advanced cooling · BSPDN

19

Power Delivery in Advanced Packages

As AI accelerators push past 700W TDP (NVIDIA B200) and toward 1000W+, power delivery through the package becomes critical.

Challenges

  • IR drop across interposer RDL and substrate
  • Simultaneous switching noise (SSN) with TB/s I/O
  • Decoupling capacitance density requirements
  • VRM-to-die impedance at >100A current

Solutions

  • eDTC — embedded Deep Trench Capacitors in interposer (CoWoS-L); high-density decoupling directly under die
  • PIVR — Package-Integrated Voltage Regulators (Marvell); VRM inside the package
  • BSPDN — Backside Power Delivery Network; separate power and signal to different die faces
  • IVR on die — Integrated voltage regulators for per-domain DVFS

TSMC BSPDN for N2: power rails on wafer backside, freeing frontside for signal routing density.

20

Thermal Management

The Heat Problem

  • 3D stacking creates buried hot spots with no direct path to heatsink
  • CTE mismatch (Si ≈ 2.6 ppm/°C vs organic ≈ 17 ppm/°C) causes warpage and solder fatigue
  • HBM stacks are thermally constrained (~1.2W per die layer, cumulative)
  • Data center racks exceed 100 kW with advanced GPU nodes

Cooling Technologies

  • Lid engineering — IHS with vapor chamber, TIM1/TIM2 optimization
  • Direct liquid cooling — cold plates on package; TSMC demonstrated 0.055 °C/W at 2.6 kW+ TDP on CoWoS
  • Microfluidic channels — etched into silicon interposer backside
  • SiC interposers — silicon carbide offers 3× the thermal conductivity of Si (exploring stage)
TSMC demonstrated direct-to-silicon liquid cooling on CoWoS at IEEE ECTC 2025, achieving 0.055 °C/W thermal resistance at 2.6 kW TDP on a 3,300 mm² interposer.

Future Directions

Panel-level packaging · Photonics · System-on-Wafer · SiC substrates

26

What's Next?

FoPLP

Fan-Out Panel-Level Packaging — move from 300 mm wafers to 600×600 mm panels. Dramatically lower cost per unit area for large packages.

Co-Packaged Optics (CPO)

Photonic transceivers integrated into the package. Eliminates electrical I/O bottlenecks for data center interconnect at 100+ Tbps per package.

System-on-Wafer (SoW)

TSMC's SoW-X architecture — entire wafer-scale integration for extreme AI/HPC. Complements Cerebras WSE approach at foundry level.

SiC Interposers

Silicon carbide as interposer material — 3× thermal conductivity of Si, better mechanical robustness. TSMC actively exploring for next-gen CoWoS.

Glass Substrates

Intel investing in glass core substrates — superior flatness, thermal stability, and ultra-fine through-glass vias (TGV) vs. organic substrates.

CoPoS

Chip on Panel on Substrate — TSMC's ultra-large-area approach complementing CoWoS for future AI/HPC packages beyond wafer-scale limits.

Summary & Key Takeaways

  • Packaging is the new scaling vector — as Moore's Law slows, advanced packaging enables continued system-level performance gains through heterogeneous integration
  • 2.5D is mainstream for HPC/AI — CoWoS and EMIB are in high-volume manufacturing, with demand exceeding supply through 2027
  • 3D stacking is production-ready — hybrid bonding enables logic-on-logic (MI300X) and will expand to logic-on-memory
  • Chiplets + UCIe enable modular SoC design — disaggregation improves yield, enables IP reuse, and allows mixing of process nodes
  • Thermal & power delivery are the binding constraints — liquid cooling, BSPDN, eDTC, and SiC substrates are all being deployed to keep pace with 700W+ TDPs
  • The supply chain matters — CoWoS capacity is the bottleneck for AI chip production; packaging facilities are receiving billions in investment worldwide
